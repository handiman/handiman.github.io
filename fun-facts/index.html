  • Stairwell cleaner in a tenement building
  • Hotel room cleaner at Hotel Foresta on Lidingö

My First Brush With Programming

In 7th grade we had something called Fritt valt ämne. You would pick a subject and explore it one hour each friday for a semester. For reasons unknown, I ended up choosing programming.

We used a Swedish school computer called Compis and wrote tiny programs in BASIC. I grasped IF statements and the infamous GOTO, but the whole thing felt abstract and slightly pointless. I remember thinking: “Who in their right mind would want to spend time doing this?”

To be fair, I was 13 and far more focused on trying to impress the girl I had a crush on than on mastering control flow.

Early Creative Adventures

Long before I became a developer, I developed all sorts of things that had nothing to do with computers:

  • Svart Duell: a home‑made Drakar och Demoner role‑playing adventure me and a friend wrote as teenagers. I still have an untouched original copy. No, it’s not for sale.
  • Sinkadus contributions: small submissions to the very first issue of the Swedish RPG magazine.

Looking back now, I’m oddly proud of these early creations. They’re rough, for sure, but they capture a younger me who just wanted to make things.
